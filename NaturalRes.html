1. Underground

  • Coal

    There is an estimated 200 billion tons of this rocky material under the Praire state. It is also helpful, but it's uses are dying out. It only really is for generating electricity and power.

  • Oil

    Illinois produces 642,000 liters of this substance a year! It is used for mostly the same purposes as coal: making electricity.

2. Animals

  • Racoon

    There are about 2,606,175 of these in Illinois. These creatures are native to the U.S. and all over. They are intelligent and love cities.

  • Little brown bat

    These are one of the 13 speces of bats in Illinois. They can eat up to 3,000 insects each night! They are also very small. (And cute!)

  • Duck

    There are 240,805 ducks in Illinois. Their feathers can be used for different things, and they lay eggs. These eggs can be used greatly. People can also hunt them for their meat.

3. Above Ground

  • Trees

    There are about 2 billion trees in this state. Even though it has many prairies, there is still a lot of forest. Its wood is used for building and making buildings and other things. You can also make paper and get its fruit.

  • Soil

    There's 1.32 MILLINON acers of this dirt. Illinois has very rich soil, so it is great with farming. It's amazing at is job: making things grow.
