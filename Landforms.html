1. Cahokia Mounds

The Cahokia mounds are large hill-like mases near Collinsville, IL. They are very big, stretching out for 2,200 acres. The mounds are manmade, created by the Missipians.The Missipians were a group who made the mounds arond the late 1200s. These hills were an important part of culture for them. Monks mound is the biggest, at 100 feet tall. Another popular mound is Mound 72, that was for burial.

As the longest river in the U.S., the Missisipi river is pretty great. The source of it is Lake Lasca, in Minnesota. It flows through Wisconsin, Iowa, Missouri, Tennessee, Kentucky, Arkansas, Michigan, and of course Illinois. It ends down in the Gulf of Mexaco. The biggest city on it is Memphis, TN. Also, it streches 2,320 miles long! This nataral stretch of water is very useful. It is used to move producs up and down the height of the U.S. But watch out! River sharks have been seen in there!
