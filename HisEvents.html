  • The Great Chicago fire


    This terrible crisis went on from Oct 8, 1871-Oct 10. 300 people were killed. 100,000 were left homeless. It spread quickly and lasted a very long time. There was a few reasons for this.

    First, it had been a very dry fall. Also, the city was made out of wood. Everything burned quickly. And the wind carried the flames all over.

    There is a legend that Mrs. O' Leary's cow kicked a lantern over in her barn. There are many more stories like this. We do know it started in her barn, in the west side of the city.

    The day before, there had been a large fire. The firemen were tired out from it. So when the fire raged on, they could barely do anything about it. Downtown and North Chicago were totaly destroyed. But for some reason, the southeast survived! It finaly stoped because of the lake and rain. 17,450 buildings were destroyed. The fire did $200 millinon worth of damage.

    But the city rebuilt. It got much bigger. By 1880, it's population was 500,000! The rebuilding was helped by donations made to Chicago. They also learned from this, making buildings not be made out of wood. The second star on it's flag repersents this event. There is now a fire academy were Mrs. O' Leary's barn was. You can see it, along with a sculpure there too.
